The premise

Deep technology is forged under pressure.

The science is hard. The timelines are long. Capital is patient or it is gone, and the people building it are usually doing several jobs at once.

What they rarely have is an advisor who has actually done all of it: someone who understands the science and the engineering, has built and scaled a company through those pressures, and can hold the boardroom and the policy room in the same conversation. Crucible Consulting is that advisor.

The difference

Most advisors have one side of this. Few have both.

Most advisors in deep tech are one of two things: a strategist who has never run a lab, or an academic who has never managed a cap table. Tom Fleming is neither.

He is an operator-scientist. Real scientific and engineering depth alongside real commercial and leadership depth, from someone who built and scaled a category-defining company rather than advising it from the outside. That is the practice's defensible edge, and everything else ladders back to it: an operator, not a career consultant; both sides of the science-and-business divide, not one; in the room, not reporting from a distance.
